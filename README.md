## 💾 Preparing the Storage Drive

The ATOTO head unit reads system updates from external storage. You must prepare your storage device correctly.

1. Insert your MicroSD card or USB drive into your Windows computer.
2. Open "File Explorer" and locate the drive icon.
3. Right-click the drive and choose "Format".
4. Select "FAT32" as the file system.
5. Click "Start" to erase the drive.
6. Open the folder containing the extracted firmware files.
7. Copy all files directly to the root directory of the drive. Do not put them inside a sub-folder.
8. Safely eject the drive from your computer once the transfer reaches one hundred percent.

## 🔧 Installing the Firmware

Take the prepared drive to your vehicle. Follow this sequence to update the system.

1. Turn on your car ignition so the ATOTO unit powers up.
2. Insert the MicroSD card or USB drive into the appropriate slot on the front of the head unit.
3. The system should detect the update files automatically.
4. A notification window appears on the screen asking for permission to update.
5. Select "Yes" or "Confirm" using the touchscreen.
6. The unit will restart and display a progress bar. 
7. Keep the vehicle power on during this process. Do not remove the drive or turn off the car.
8. The unit will restart again once the installation finishes.
9. Remove the drive from the slot.

## 🔍 Troubleshooting Tips

If you do not see a prompt after inserting the drive, verify these points:

* Ensure you used a FAT32 file system format on your drive.
* Check that you copied the files to the top level of the drive, not inside a folder.
* Confirm that the downloaded file matched your specific model number.
* Try a different USB port or a different card if the system fails to recognize the data.

The system might reset your personal configurations like radio presets and saved Bluetooth devices. You should write down your settings before you start the update. The update does not change your hardware, so your steering wheel controls and backup camera settings should work immediately after the process finishes.

If the unit fails to boot after the update, perform a hard reset. Locate the tiny reset hole on the front panel of the S8 unit. Use a paperclip to press the button inside for five seconds. This forces the Android operating system to reboot its core services.
